e·mo /ˈēmō/

noun — a style of rock music resembling punk but having more complex arrangements and lyrics that deal with more emotional subjects.

A Brief History.

One of the most frequently discussed topics regarding Emo music is its origins and how it came to be. It’s essentially become common knowledge after hearing it so frequently, my music elitists, and as wiki says, “Emo (/ˈiːmoʊ/) is a genre of rock music characterized by emotional, often confessional lyrics. It emerged as a style of hardcore punk and post-hardcore from the mid-1980s Washington, D.C., hardcore scene, where it was known as emotional hardcore or emocore.” The most important element of how emo came to be is what the influences because other genres will continue to mold and shape Emo for many years to come, following its humble beginnings.
